Project Description

The ethics reviewer project involved the creation of an interactive learning tool to be used in a case study by UCL law professor Richard Moorhead. The case involved is the Leveson Inquiry which is looked into the culture, practices and ethics of the British press following the News International phone hacking scandal. Interactive web tool will provide a mechanism for delivering the information, which will be in the form of videos and text while posing multiple choice questions for the students to answer. The idea is that these questions will be answered in real time (during the lecture) by all the students so that the lecturer can immediately see the types of responses and clarify and misunderstandings. Additionally, the web application is expected to take the form of a timeline. This timeline will chronologically follow the key events in the case, showing different stimuli and posing questions as it goes along.
